Providing a way for you to share your shopping experience with others in a virtual platform.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

A mobile application built for e-commerce.

WeShop was created to combine the convenience and ease of e-commerce with the human experience that took place when shopping with your best friend or family members.

The initial idea for WeShop was inspired by noticing all the different tools that family members and friends were using to shop for one another.

Thus began our quest to create an application that combined all of the individual components into one platform, while maintaining an intuitive experience.

THE PROBLEM

1.

The Experience Has Been Lost

E-commerce and virtual shopping has dominated and is the preferred purchasing method; which is great for convenience and price comparison, but terrible for the experience.

2.

Multi-Platform Shopping

Many individuals resort to combining different tools and applications such as FaceTime, Notes appm, third party registry applicaitons, and text messages as their means to virtually shop.

THE SOLUTION

1.

Bring The Experience To E-Commerce

Combine the convenience and ease of e-commerce, with the human experience that takes place when shopping with your best friend or family.

2.

A Cohesive Experience

Incorporate all of the pre-existing features that other companies and products provide, and additionally create a cohesive experience for users who want to shop virtually.

Target User

1.

Individuals 13-32 Years Old

According to our interviews, we found that this age demographic is most influenced by Family/Friend Recommendations, Online Reviews, and Influencers. They also see themselves as trendsetters, which helps our early adoption.

2.

Parents 50+ Years Old

Despite low figures in the data, this group surprisingly revealed during their interviews, much of their purchasing habits were a direct result of their children and family. This group is a direct dependent on our target user.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

The next step in our research process was to perform a competitor analysis to look at what other companies are currently doing and if there are similar applications on the market. We looked at Amazon Wishlist, Instagram Store, Zola, Verishop, and Squadded.


From these companies, we noticed a general trend of registry-like services, however, it did not fulfill our original purpose statement for an emphasis on social e-commerce and all-in-one application.

USER FLOW

At this point, we were ready to create a user flow that outlined the steps and walkthrough process of the application. We focused on the main components and features that would be included to complete WeShop. The features we wanted to focus on were the wishlists (personal, friend, and group lists), barcode scanner, and a swipe feature on the homepage.

HOW IT WORKS

Low-fidelity Wireframes

At the beginning of the design process, low-fidelity wireframes were created for testing purposes and design inspiration for future high-fidelity wireframes.


As these wireframes were the initial mockup for the application, there were many iterations to the first design. The low-fidelity wireframes incoporated the main features that were considered main interactions and pages, however, with later iterations, smaller micro-interactions were added to improve the ease and experience.

BRANDING

UI Style Guide

With our initial low fidelity wireframes designed, the next step was to create a style guide and focus our brand on creating a fun environment to shop with others online.

OUTCOME

High-fidelity Wireframes

After reviewing the initial wireframe with the rest of the team, the next step was to transform the wireframe to the high-fidelity prototype. During this process, additional pages and features had to be added in order to account for gaps within the low-fidelity wireframes and overall cohesiveness of navigating the applicaiton.

What I've Learned

As this was one of my first projects as Lead UX/UI Designer, I had to critically think through every aspect of this design and research in order to ensure all bases were covered. Rather than in design challenges or personal projects, in which deliverables are established preemptively, I had to take it into my own hands to layout the proper design principles and methods to use to complete the project.

Next Steps

The high-fidelity prototype is the initial design for WeShop and we have additional features that we would like to add in the future. This includes a video chat split screen feature which would allow users to shop virtually and in-person with their friends and family. To expand WeShop, we'd like to add a browser extension to be able to save products to a wishlist while browsing on a desktop. We would also like to begin reaching out to brands to participate in the swipe list as this would provide data and research for product user testing.
